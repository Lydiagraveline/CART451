Jeff Howe, "The Rise Of Crowdsourcing" Lauren McCarthy: social turkers:crowdsourced dating (2013)t Kyle McDonald: Exhausting a Crowd (2015)

Jeff Howe's "The Rise of Crowdsourcing” describes how crowdsourcing, through companies like iStockphoto or Amazon’s Mechanical Turk, has disrupted traditional industries and allowed companies to leverage/take advantage of the collective power of the crowd. This concept is explored in Lauren McCarthy's project, where she harnesses the power of crowdsourced workers (using the Mechanical Turk) who make real-time social decisions for her while shes on a date. Her project involves sharing intimate moments with remote workers, highlighting the potential intrusion of privacy in the age of technologicaly-mediated daily life. Another project involving an online audience is McDonald's "Exhausting a Crowd," which invites anyone via the internet to write comments on surveillance footage of public spaces. These projects demonstrate how technology can be used to connect individuals to larger, often remote, audiences, and used to facilitate collective participation in creative endeavors. They bring into mind ethical dilemmas brought about by the increasing role of remote audiences in our lives, shaping our interactions and daily life in unprecedented ways, blurring the boundaries between private and public space.
